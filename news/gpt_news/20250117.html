Keeta Privacy Morning Coffee

Date: January 17, 2025

Section 1: Key Privacy Compliance Developments

Privacy Compliance, Data Security, Data Breaches, and Regulatory Updates

  • EU Files Privacy Complaints Against Chinese Firms
    The Austrian advocacy group Noyb has filed privacy complaints against six Chinese companies, including TikTok, Shein, and Xiaomi, alleging unlawful transmission of EU user data to China. These complaints could result in fines up to 4% of global revenue. Under the EU's GDPR, data transfers to countries with lower data protection standards, like China, are prohibited.
    REUTERS
    Analysis & Recommendations: Companies operating within the EU must ensure compliance with GDPR, particularly concerning cross-border data transfers. Implementing standard contractual clauses and conducting thorough data protection impact assessments are essential to mitigate regulatory risks.
  • China Vows Crackdown on Illegal Data Handling
    China's state planner has announced efforts to crack down on illegal data handling, targeting black and grey markets that unlawfully obtain, sell, or provide data. New regulations aim to improve data security governance, emphasizing the need to monitor data security risks, particularly in key industries. The goal is to prevent systemic and large-scale risks to maintain national security and social stability.
    REUTERS
    Analysis & Recommendations: Organizations operating in China should review their data handling practices to ensure compliance with the new regulations. Establishing robust data governance frameworks and conducting regular audits can help prevent unauthorized data transactions and enhance overall data security.
  • US Finalizes Rule to Ban Chinese Vehicles with Connected Software
    The Biden administration has finalized a rule effectively banning the sale or import of vehicles with connected software from countries of concern, including China. This measure addresses national security risks posed by technology like cameras, sensors, and onboard computers that could be exploited to collect sensitive data. The ban will take effect for model year 2027 vehicles for software and model year 2030 for hardware.
    THE VERGE
    Analysis & Recommendations: Automotive companies should assess their supply chains and technology partnerships to ensure compliance with the new regulations. Exploring alternative suppliers and enhancing cybersecurity measures will be crucial to mitigate potential disruptions and safeguard sensitive data.

Section 2: Industry Insights

Food Delivery and Chinese Outbound Business Developments

  • NYC's Data Sharing Law Declared Unconstitutional
    A federal judge ruled that a New York City law requiring food delivery companies to share customer data with restaurants violated the First Amendment by improperly regulating commercial speech. This decision underscores the delicate balance between data sharing practices and constitutional rights.
    NEW YORK POST
    Analysis & Recommendations: Food delivery platforms should reassess their data sharing policies to ensure they align with legal standards and protect customer privacy. Implementing transparent data practices and obtaining explicit customer consent can help maintain trust and avoid legal challenges.
  • China Issues New Regulations on Network Data Security Management
    On September 30, 2024, China's State Council introduced the new Network Data Security Management Regulations, which will come into effect on January 1, 2025. These regulations aim to address the increasing challenges of data security by providing a legal framework for managing network data processing activities. They set out comprehensive guidelines on personal data protection, cross-border data transfers, and the responsibilities of internet platform providers.
    STATE COUNCIL OF CHINA
    Analysis & Recommendations: Businesses operating in China should familiarize themselves with the new regulations and adjust their data processing practices accordingly. Establishing comprehensive data protection measures and ensuring compliance with cross-border data transfer requirements will be essential to maintain regulatory compliance and consumer trust.
